# Money Models — Cash-Flow Architecture

Use this guide when acquisition works but cash recovery limits growth. It is a paraphrased operating guide. Retrieve attribution from `references/evidence/money-models.md`.

## Core definitions

- **CAC:** Fully loaded marketing and sales cost per acquired customer.
- **Gross profit:** Collected revenue minus direct fulfillment COGS.
- **Payback:** Earliest point when cumulative gross profit equals or exceeds CAC.
- **LTGP:** Lifetime gross profit from one customer.

Current standards used by this skill:

- Bare-minimum client-financed acquisition: `first-30-day gross profit > CAC`.
- Preferred standard: `first-30-day gross profit > 2 x CAC`.

The inequality is strict. Gross profit has already removed COGS, so do not subtract COGS again.

Older material used `30-day cash collected > 2 x (CAC + COGS)`. Keep it separate and label it historical when relevant.

## Sequence offers

A money model is a planned sequence, not a collection of unrelated products:

1. **Attraction offer:** Convert a stranger into a paying customer with low perceived risk.
2. **Upsell:** Solve the next problem created or revealed by the first purchase.
3. **Downsell:** Reshape scope, payment, or commitment after a no without disguising the same offer.
4. **Continuity:** Charge recurring money for recurring value.

Prefer more ways to sell the same core solution over adding operationally unrelated products.

## Design levers

- Move payment earlier when the value is delivered early.
- Match billing cadence to ongoing value.
- Use annual or longer commitments only when the customer receives a fair exchange.
- Present the next relevant offer while purchase intent is high.
- Preserve price integrity by changing terms, scope, timing, or bonuses before discounting.
- Increase gross margin through better delivery design, not by quietly degrading outcomes.

## Diagnose the constraint

| Symptom | Likely issue |
|---|---|
| Strong lifetime economics, slow payback | Timing or offer-sequence problem |
| Weak first-month retention | Delivery problem before money-model optimization |
| High CAC with weak conversion | Market, offer, lead, or sales problem |
| GP:CAC below 1x | Acquisition is not client-financed |
| GP:CAC between 1x and 2x | Self-funding but still cash constrained |
| GP:CAC above 2x with capacity | Candidate for controlled scaling |

## Completion check

A money-model recommendation is complete when it shows the accounting with units, identifies the timing or sequence bottleneck, and changes one offer or payment event without mixing cash and gross-profit formulas.
