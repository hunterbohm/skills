# Offers — Design, Pricing, and Risk Reversal

Use this guide to diagnose whether the market or offer is preventing sales. It is a paraphrased operating guide. Retrieve attribution from `references/evidence/offers.md`.

## Diagnostic order

1. **Market:** Confirm pain, purchasing power, targetability, and sufficient demand.
2. **Offer:** Make the outcome specific, credible, fast, and easier to achieve.
3. **Sales:** Improve persuasion only after the first two are credible.

Treat repeated objections that the offer is generic, risky, slow, effortful, or not worth the price as an offer problem before treating them as a closer problem.

## Value equation

`Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)`

- Raise the dream outcome by making the result concrete and important.
- Raise perceived likelihood with proof, specificity, a credible mechanism, and fit.
- Reduce time delay by moving the first meaningful win earlier.
- Reduce effort and sacrifice by simplifying delivery and removing friction.

The denominator is often the stronger differentiator. A believable, faster, easier path usually beats a larger unsupported claim.

## Build the offer

1. Define one buyer and one painful result.
2. List every obstacle between the buyer and that result.
3. Turn each obstacle into a solution.
4. Choose delivery methods that preserve the result without unnecessary cost.
5. Remove low-value complexity.
6. Bundle the remaining pieces around one clear outcome.
7. Name the offer for the buyer, goal, time horizon, and delivery container when useful.

Use bonuses to remove specific objections or accelerate the result. Do not add unrelated volume merely to make the stack look larger.

## Pricing

- Price against the value of the result, not only fulfillment cost or competitors.
- Raise value before raising price.
- Prefer a differentiated premium offer over discount competition.
- Treat extremely high close rates as a possible underpricing signal, not proof by themselves.
- Preserve margin needed for strong fulfillment, talent, support, and acquisition.

## Guarantees

Choose the narrowest risk reversal the business can honor:

- **Unconditional:** Appropriate only when abuse and fulfillment exposure are controlled.
- **Conditional:** Tie relief to customer actions the business can verify.
- **Service-level:** Continue or repeat work when a defined process milestone is missed.
- **Anti-guarantee:** State clearly why no refund promise is appropriate.

Guarantee controllable process or service commitments. Do not guarantee medical, legal, financial, employment, admissions, or other outcomes controlled by third parties or uncertain events.

## Completion check

An offer recommendation is complete when it identifies the weak value lever, proposes one measurable change, and explains how that change should alter conversion, price, speed, or delivery risk.
