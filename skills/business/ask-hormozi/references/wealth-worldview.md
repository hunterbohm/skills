# Wealth and Worldview — Ownership, Cash Flow, and Time Horizon

Use this guide when the user asks about Hormozi's public wealth philosophy, ownership, investing priorities, selling a company, reputation, or long-term games. It is a paraphrased operating guide. Retrieve attribution from `references/evidence/wealth-worldview.md`.

This is a worldview translator, not personalized investment advice. Do not turn general business philosophy into a recommendation to buy, sell, borrow, concentrate, or avoid diversification without the user's financial facts and appropriate professional review.

## Core sequence

The public philosophy generally follows this order:

1. Build valuable skills.
2. Apply them to increase earned cash flow.
3. Build or own an operating asset.
4. Reinvest where the operator has knowledge and control.
5. Diversify later to protect accumulated wealth.

Treat concentration as a description of entrepreneurial wealth creation, not a universal portfolio rule.

## Cash flow and ownership

- Revenue measures activity; profit and cash determine survival.
- Ownership can compound beyond what salary alone produces.
- A cash-flow business and a high-growth enterprise-value business should not be judged by the same exit logic.
- Selling an asset exchanges future control and cash flow for liquidity now.
- Evaluate an exit against the after-tax proceeds, lost cash flow, risk removed, future opportunity, and the owner's desired role.

## Capital allocation

Use a hierarchy of advantage:

- **Skills:** Often highest leverage when income is low and capability is the bottleneck.
- **Own business:** Attractive when the operator has control, evidence, and reinvestment capacity.
- **External assets:** Useful for diversification, liquidity, and risks the operator should not concentrate.

Do not infer that one level is always superior. Expected return, downside, liquidity, taxes, time, and personal concentration all matter.

## Time and leverage

Leverage increases output per unit of input through labor, capital, software, media, systems, or reputation. Favor durable assets and capabilities over status consumption when the stated goal is long-term wealth.

Use a long time horizon to capture compounding, but pair patience with periodic evidence checks. Longevity in a bad vehicle is not a strategy.

## Reputation and goodwill

Treat kept promises, fair dealing, useful public work, and customer outcomes as compounding assets. Reputation increases trust and future opportunity only when behavior continues to support it.

## Completion check

A wealth-worldview answer is complete when it separates philosophy from personalized advice, identifies the relevant ownership or cash-flow tradeoff, and gives one decision criterion rather than a portfolio prescription.
