### Attraction Offer  _(aka Attraction Offers, Front-end offer)_
The first, low-risk offer whose only job is to turn a cold stranger into a paying customer.
> “Number one is you have attraction offers.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Making Money is a Game (Here's the Cheat Code)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nSQdjim8CsE) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA)  _(aka CFA, Client-financed acquisition, Self-funding growth loop)_
Making a new customer's early gross profit repay their fully loaded acquisition cost, so growth can fund itself.
**Source:** [Acquisition.com — CFA](https://www.acquisition.com/training/money/cfa) · paraphrase only; no exact linked excerpt shipped · confidence: high

### Continuity  _(aka Continuity Offers, Recurring revenue offer)_
An offer that charges ongoing money for ongoing value — recurring revenue you sell once and get paid for repeatedly.

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)  _(aka CAC, Cost to acquire a customer)_
The full cost — ads plus sales — to turn a prospect into one paying customer.
**Source:** [Million Dollar Equations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QGcjweehrvU) · paraphrase only; no exact linked excerpt shipped · confidence: high

### Downsell  _(aka Downsell Offer, Downsells)_
The alternative offer you make after a customer says 'no,' reshaped to fit their budget or commitment.
> “And so by doing that, we turn nos into yeses.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Making Money is a Game (Here's the Cheat Code)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nSQdjim8CsE) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### Gross Profit (as Hormozi uses it)  _(aka Gross profit, Gross margin)_
What's left from a sale after only the cost to make and deliver it — explicitly not net profit or revenue.
**Formula:** `Gross Profit = Revenue - Cost of delivering the product/service`
**Source:** The Game w/ Alex Hormozi — Ep 860: Why Most Businesses Stall After $3M · paraphrase only; no exact linked excerpt shipped · confidence: high

### Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)  _(aka LTGP, Lifetime gross profit)_
The total gross profit one customer produces over their whole relationship with you — the real reinvestable cash, not revenue or LTV.
**Formula:** `LTGP = Gross Profit per purchase x Avg. purchases  (recurring: Gross Profit / Churn %)`
Components: Gross Profit; Number of transactions or Churn rate
> “Business actually has a term for this, which is the lifetime gross profit, which is LTGP.”
— Alex Hormozi, [How I Would Build a Business in 2026 (If I Had to Start Over)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i7bLRKwKSms) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### LTGP:CAC Ratio  _(aka LTGP to CAC ratio, LTV:CAC ratio, LTV:CAC, LGP to CAC, Lifetime Gross Profit to Customer Acquisition Cost)_
How many dollars of lifetime gross profit you earn per dollar spent acquiring a customer — the health check for a business model.
**Formula:** `LTGP / CAC  (~3:1 unlimited-scale; +3 per human in the loop)`
Components: 3:1 = healthy floor / no human (unlimited scale); 6:1 = one human in the loop; 9:1 = two humans in the loop; 12:1 = three humans in the loop
> “This is the fundamental economic unit of the business.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Million Dollar Equations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QGcjweehrvU) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### Money Model  _(aka Money Models, Offer Sequence)_
A deliberate, pre-planned sequence of offers that gets a customer's acquisition cost back fast — not a single sale.
> “A money model is a series of offers deliberately structured to generate the most upfront cash.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Making Money is a Game (Here's the Cheat Code)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nSQdjim8CsE) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high
**Official framework reference:** [Acquisition.com Money Models course](https://www.acquisition.com/training/money/context)

### Nuisance Businesses
Businesses customers know they should use but keep deprioritizing — reputation maintenance, SEO, etc.
**Source:** [81 Minutes of Money Making Advice You Needed to Know Yesterday](https://rosetta.to/u/alexhormozi/81-minutes-of-money-making-advice-you-needed-to-know-yesterday) · paraphrase only; no exact linked excerpt shipped · confidence: medium

### Payback Period (30-Day Rule)  _(aka Payback window, PPD, Payback period, CAC Payback, Self-Funding Growth)_
How long it takes to earn back, in gross profit, what you spent to acquire a customer.
**Formula:** `Payback = earliest time t where cumulative gross profit through t >= CAC`
> “Payback period, which is how quickly can you recover the cash that you spent before you can make that money.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Business is hard until you do this](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CoPs-Bk8M9Y) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### Size of the Pie Fallacy
The false belief that a competitor entering your channel shrinks your share of a fixed market.
> “size of the pie fallacy.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Your biggest advantage is no one knows who you are](https://rosetta.to/u/alexhormozi/your-biggest-advantage-is-no-one-knows-who-you-are) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### Smash and Grab Businesses  _(aka Smashing grab businesses)_
Pure media-arbitrage businesses that spike fast, have no brand or delivery moat, and collapse when the arbitrage closes.
> “this is the real nasty one because if you just have a pure I call them smash and grab businesses…”

### The 30-Day 2x CAC Rule  _(aka 30-day gross profit exceeds 2x CAC, The '$100M Money Model' standard, Double acquisition cost in 30 days)_
Aim for a new customer's first-30-day gross profit to exceed twice their fully loaded customer-acquisition cost.
**Formula:** `30-Day Gross Profit > 2 x CAC`
> “You make more money getting a customer than it costs you to get them within the first 30 days.”
— Alex Hormozi, [Making Money is a Game (Here's the Cheat Code)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nSQdjim8CsE) · exact linked excerpt · confidence: high

### The Pie Equation  _(aka Pi equation)_
Every business grows by only three levers: more customers, bigger transactions, or more purchases over time.
