# Sales — Belief, Diagnosis, and Process

Use this guide when qualified opportunities exist but too few buy, attend, or complete follow-up. It is a paraphrased operating guide. Retrieve attribution from `references/evidence/sales.md`.

## Diagnose before prescribing

Separate four failure modes:

- **Offer:** Prospects repeatedly describe the offer as generic, risky, slow, effortful, or not worth the price.
- **Qualification:** Calls contain people without the problem, authority, urgency, or resources.
- **Process:** Slow response, low attendance, weak follow-up, or inconsistent call review.
- **Closing:** Qualified prospects understand the offer but leave with unresolved uncertainty.

Do not use sales technique to hide a weak offer or poor fit.

## CLOSER sequence

Use the sequence as a diagnostic conversation, not a script to overpower the buyer:

1. **Clarify:** Learn why the prospect took the conversation and what result matters.
2. **Label:** Summarize the problem in the prospect's language and confirm it.
3. **Overview:** Understand prior attempts, costs, and why they failed.
4. **Sell the outcome:** Connect the offer to the desired destination rather than reciting features.
5. **Explain concerns:** Resolve uncertainty with relevant evidence and honest boundaries.
6. **Reinforce:** After purchase, confirm the decision and next step to reduce remorse.

## Handle objections

Treat an objection as missing certainty or fit information.

Use the 3A pattern:

1. **Acknowledge** the concern without arguing.
2. **Associate** it with a legitimate decision criterion or prior buyer pattern.
3. **Ask** a question that isolates the actual blocker.

If the blocker is real, change the offer, terms, timing, or recommendation. Do not manufacture urgency, conceal material facts, or pressure a prospect who should not buy.

## Process metrics

Track the entire path:

- lead response time;
- contact rate;
- booked-to-attended rate;
- qualified rate;
- close rate on qualified opportunities;
- cash collected;
- refunds, cancellations, and early customer success.

Review recordings or transcripts against a short rubric. Improve one observed behavior at a time. Pair conversion metrics with refund, churn, and success metrics so the team cannot optimize sales at the customer's expense.

## Completion check

A sales recommendation is complete when it identifies the failing stage, supports it with one metric or repeated buyer signal, and assigns one observable behavior to test.
